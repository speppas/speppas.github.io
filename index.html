About

Welcome! I am currently a third-year PhD student at the Electrical & Computer Engineering Department of UVA, where I have the privilege of working with Prof. Nicholas Sidiropoulos. My research focuses on wireless communications, with a particular emphasis on signal detection and time synchronization using (interference-resilient) subspace methods.

Before coming to Virginia, I earned my BSc and integrated MEng from the ECE department of the Technical University of Crete. During this time, I had the opportunity to work with Prof. Aggelos Bletsas, concentrating on RFID localization using compressive sensing and maximum likelihood approaches. You can find my diploma thesis here. I also worked on Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS), where we prototyped the first wireless and batteryless surface using (passive) RFID tags as elements [link to story].

For more information, you can take a look at my CV.

Currently looking for an internship for the summer of 2025.
